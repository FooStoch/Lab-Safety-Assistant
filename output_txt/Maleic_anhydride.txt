Maleic anhydride – Formula: C4H2O3 (CAS 108-31-6) - Synonyms/Trade Names: cis-Butenedioic anhydride; 2,5-Furanedione; Maleic acid anhydride; Toxilic anhydride. IDLH 10 mg/m3 | NIOSH REL: TWA 1 mg/m3 (0.25 ppm) OSHA PEL: TWA 1 mg/m3 (0.25 ppm) | Incompatibilities and Reactivities: Strong oxidizers, water, alkalis, metals, caustics, and amines above 150°F [Note: Reacts slowly with water (hydrolyzes) to form maleic acid.] Exposure Routes, Symptoms, Target Organs (see Table 5): ER: Inh, Ing, Con SY: Irrit nose, upper resp sys; conj; photo, double vision; bronchial asthma; derm TO: Eyes, skin, resp sys First Aid (see Table 6): Eye: Irr immed Skin: Soap wash immed Breath: Resp support Swallow: Medical attention immed 190 M Malonaldehyde. Personal Protection/Sanitation: (see Table 2): Skin: Prevent skin contact Eyes: Prevent eye contact Wash skin: When contam Remove: When wet or contam Change: N.R. Provide: Eyewash. Physical Description: Colorless needles, white lumps, or pellets with an irritating, choking odor..
