Chlorine – Formula: Cl2 (CAS 7782-50-5) - Synonyms/Trade Names: Molecular chlorine. IDLH 10 ppm | NIOSH REL: C 0.5 ppm (1.45 mg/m3 ) [15-minute] OSHA PEL†: C 1 ppm (3 mg/m3 ) | Incompatibilities and Reactivities: Reacts explosively or forms explosive compounds with many common substances such as acetylene, ether, turpentine, ammonia, fuel gas, hydrogen & finely divided metals. Exposure Routes, Symptoms, Target Organs (see Table 5): ER: Inh, Con SY: Burning of eyes, nose, mouth; lac, rhin; cough, choking, subs pain; nau, vomit; head, dizz; syncope; pulm edema; pneu; hypox; derm; liquid: frostbite TO: Eyes, skin, resp sys First Aid (see Table 6): Eye: Frostbite Skin: Frostbite Breath: Resp support C Chlorine dioxide. Personal Protection/Sanitation: (see Table 2): Skin: Frostbite Eyes: Frostbite Wash skin: N.R. Remove: N.R. Change: N.R. Provide: Frostbite wash. Physical Description: Greenish-yellow gas with a pungent, irritating odor. [Note: Shipped as a liquefied compressed gas.].
