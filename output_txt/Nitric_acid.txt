Nitric acid – Formula: HNO3 (CAS 7697-37-2) - Synonyms/Trade Names: Aqua fortis, Engravers acid, Hydrogen nitrate, Red fuming nitric acid (RFNA), White fuming nitric acid (WFNA). IDLH 25 ppm | NIOSH REL: TWA 2 ppm (5 mg/m3 ) ST 4 ppm (10 mg/m3 ) OSHA PEL†: TWA 2 ppm (5 mg/m3 ) | Incompatibilities and Reactivities: Combustible materials, metallic powders, hydrogen sulfide, carbides, alcohols [Note: Reacts with water to produce heat. Personal Protection/Sanitation: (see Table 2): Skin: Prevent skin contact Eyes: Prevent eye contact Wash skin: When contam Remove: When wet or contam Change: N.R. Provide: Eyewash (pH<2.5) Quick drench (pH<2.5). Physical Description: Colorless, yellow, or red, fuming liquid with an acrid, suffocating odor. [Note: Often used in an aqueous solution. Fuming nitric acid is concentrated nitric acid that contains dissolved nitrogen dioxide.].
