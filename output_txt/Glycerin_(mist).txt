Glycerin (mist) – Formula: HOCH2CH(OH)CH2OH (CAS 56-81-5) - Synonyms/Trade Names: Glycerin (anhydrous); Glycerol; Glycyl alcohol; 1,2,3-Propanetriol; Trihydroxypropane. IDLH N.D. | NIOSH REL: See Appendix D OSHA PEL†: TWA 15 mg/m3 (total) TWA 5 mg/m3 (resp) | Incompatibilities and Reactivities: Strong oxidizers (e.g., chromium trioxide, potassium chlorate, potassium permanganate) [Note: Hygroscopic (i.e., absorbs moisture from the air).] Exposure Routes, Symptoms, Target Organs (see Table 5): ER: Inh, Con SY: Irrit eyes, skin, resp sys; head, nau, vomit; kidney inj TO: Eyes, skin, resp sys, kidneys First Aid (see Table 6): Eye: Irr immed Skin: Water wash Breath: Fresh air 152 Glycidol. Personal Protection/Sanitation: (see Table 2): Skin: N.R. Eyes: N.R. Wash skin: N.R. Remove: N.R. Change: N.R.. Physical Description: Clear, colorless, odorless, syrupy liquid or solid (below 64°F). [Note: The solid form melts above 64°F but the liquid form freezes at a much lower temperature.].
