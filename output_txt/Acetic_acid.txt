Acetic acid – Formula: CH3COOH (CAS 64-19-7) - Synonyms/Trade Names: Acetic acid (aqueous), Ethanoic acid, Glacial acetic acid (pure compound), Methanecarboxylic acid [Note: Can be found in concentrations of 5-8% in vinegar.]. IDLH 50 ppm | NIOSH REL: TWA 10 ppm (25 mg/m3 ) OSHA PEL: TWA 10 ppm (25 mg/m3 ) ST 15 ppm (37 mg/m3 ) | Incompatibilities and Reactivities: Strong oxidizers (especially chromic acid, sodium peroxide & nitric acid), strong caustics [Note: Corrosive to metals.] Exposure Routes, Symptoms, Target Organs (see Table 5): ER: Inh, Con SY: Irrit eyes, skin, nose, throat; eye, skin burns; skin sens; dental erosion; black skin, hyperkeratosis; conj, lac; phar edema, chronic bron TO: Eyes, skin, resp sys, teeth First Aid (see Table 6): Eye: Irr immed Skin: Water flush immed Breath: Resp support Swallow: Medical attention immed 2 A Acetic anhydride. Personal Protection/Sanitation: (see Table 2): Skin: Prevent skin contact (>10%) Eyes: Prevent eye contact Wash skin: When contam (>10%) Remove: When wet or contam (>10%) Change: N.R. Provide: Eyewash (>5%) Quick drench (>50%). Physical Description: Colorless liquid or crystals with a sour, vinegar-like odor. [Note: Pure compound is a solid below 62°F. Often used in an aqueous solution.].
