Resorcinol – Formula: C6H4(OH)2 (CAS 108-46-3) - Synonyms/Trade Names: 1,3-Benzenediol; m-Benzenediol; 1,3-Dihydroxybenzene; m-Dihydroxybenzene; 3-Hydroxyphenol; m-Hydroxyphenol. IDLH N.D. | NIOSH REL: TWA 10 ppm (45 mg/m3 ) ST 20 ppm (90 mg/m3 ) OSHA PEL†: none | Incompatibilities and Reactivities: Acetanilide, albumin, alkalis, antipyrine, camphor, ferric salts, menthol, spirit nitrous ether, strong oxidizers & bases [Note: Hygroscopic (i.e., absorbs moisture from the air).] Exposure Routes, Symptoms, Target Organs (see Table 5): ER: Inh, Ing, Con SY: Irrit eyes, skin, nose, throat, upper resp sys; methemo; cyan, convuls; restless, bluish skin, incr heart rate, dysp; dizz, drow, hypothermia, hema; spleen, kidney, liver changes; derm TO: Eyes, skin, resp sys, CVS, CNS, blood, spleen, liver, kidneys First Aid (see Table 6): Eye: Irr immed Skin: Water wash immed Breath: Resp support Swallow: Medical attention immed Rhodium (metal fume and insoluble compounds, as Rh). Personal Protection/Sanitation: (see Table 2): Skin: Prevent skin contact Eyes: Prevent eye contact Wash skin: When contam Remove: When wet or contam Change: Daily Provide: Eyewash. Physical Description: White needles, plates, crystals, flakes, or powder with a faint odor. [Note: Turns pink on exposure to air or light, or contact with iron.] Measurement Methods (see Table 1): NIOSH 5701 OSHA PV2053.
