Carbon dioxide – Formula: CO2 (CAS 124-38-9) - Synonyms/Trade Names: Carbonic acid gas, Dry ice [Note: Normal constituent of air (about 300 ppm)].. IDLH 40,000 ppm | NIOSH REL: TWA 5000 ppm (9000 mg/m3 ) ST 30,000 ppm (54,000 mg/m3 ) OSHA PEL†: TWA 5000 ppm (9000 mg/m3 ) | Incompatibilities and Reactivities: Dusts of various metals, such as magnesium, zirconium, titanium, aluminum, chromium & manganese are ignitable and explosive when suspended in carbon dioxide. Forms carbonic acid in water. Exposure Routes, Symptoms, Target Organs (see Table 5): ER: Inh, Con (liquid/solid) SY: Head, dizz, restless, pares; dysp; sweat, mal; incr heart rate, card output, BP; coma; asphy; convuls; frostbite (liq, dry ice) TO: Resp sys, CVS First Aid (see Table 6): Eye: Frostbite Skin: Frostbite Breath: Resp support C Carbon disulfide. Personal Protection/Sanitation: (see Table 2): Skin: Frostbite Eyes: Frostbite Wash skin: N.R. Remove: N.R. Change: N.R. Provide: Frostbite wash. Physical Description: Colorless, odorless gas. [Note: Shipped as a liquefied compressed gas. Solid form is utilized as dry ice.].
